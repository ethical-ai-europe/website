## Why Community Matters

### Share Experiences

When you've dealt with an AI system‚Äîwhether you successfully challenged a decision or hit a wall‚Äîyour experience helps others. What worked? What didn't? What would you do differently?

### Learn from Others

Someone else has probably faced what you're facing. Their insights can save you time, help you avoid mistakes, and give you ideas you wouldn't have had on your own.

### Collective Voice

One person asking questions is easy to ignore. A community demanding answers is not. Together, we can push for transparency, accountability, and respect for rights.

### Stay Connected

AI governance is evolving fast. Being part of a community keeps you connected to developments, opportunities, and people who share your concerns.

## Community Guidelines

Our community is built on mutual respect and shared purpose. To keep it welcoming and useful for everyone:

### ü§ù Respect and Inclusion

- Treat everyone with respect, regardless of their background or expertise level
- Welcome newcomers and help them get started
- Recognize that people have different experiences and perspectives
- Use inclusive language and be mindful of cultural differences

### üìö Evidence-Based Discussion

- Support claims with sources when possible
- Distinguish between fact, analysis, and opinion
- Be open to updating your views based on new information
- Respect expertise while encouraging questions

### üîß Constructive Engagement

- Focus on solutions, not just problems
- Offer specific, actionable suggestions
- Disagree respectfully and seek to understand
- Help others, even when it doesn't directly benefit you

### üö´ What's Not Welcome

- Personal attacks or harassment of any kind
- Commercial promotion or spam
- Misinformation or unverified claims presented as fact
- Content that undermines the rights we're trying to protect
- Illegal advice or encouragement of illegal activity

*Full community guidelines are available when you join.*
