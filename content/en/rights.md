# Your AI Rights in Europe

**You have rights.** Whether it's an algorithm deciding your loan application, AI screening your job resume, or facial recognition tracking you in public spaces‚ÄîEuropean law gives you the power to understand, question, and challenge these systems.

This isn't about understanding complex regulations. It's about knowing what you can do when AI affects your life.

## What Can I Do Right Now?

If you think AI has been used in a decision that affects you:

**1. Ask if AI was involved**
You have the right to know. Ask directly: "Was any automated system or algorithm involved in this decision?"

**2. Request an explanation**
If AI was used, ask for a clear explanation of how the decision was made and what factors were considered.

**3. Ask for human review**
If you're unhappy with an automated decision, request that a human reviews your case. This is especially important for significant decisions.

**4. Document everything**
Keep records of your communications and any decisions you receive. This helps if you need to challenge something later.

**5. Know where to complain**
If an organization won't respect your rights, you can contact your national data protection authority or consumer protection agency.

## The Legal Foundation

Your rights come from two landmark pieces of European legislation:

**The EU AI Act** (2024) is the world's first comprehensive AI law. It bans certain AI practices entirely and sets strict requirements for high-risk AI systems.

**The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** (2018) gives you rights over your personal data, including the right to know about and challenge automated decisions.

These laws work together to protect you. You don't need to understand every detail‚Äîjust know that the law is on your side.
